Research Overview

Overview

AICAR is widely studied as an activator of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), a central regulator of cellular energy homeostasis. Researchers investigate how AMPK activation influences metabolism, endurance physiology, glucose utilization, and lipid metabolism.

Because AMPK acts as an intracellular energy sensor, AICAR continues to be explored in laboratory studies involving metabolic health and exercise adaptation.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

AICAR is converted inside cells into a molecule capable of activating AMPK. Scientists investigate how this signaling pathway regulates energy production, nutrient utilization, and metabolic adaptation.
