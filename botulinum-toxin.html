Research Overview

Overview

Botulinum toxin is a bacterial neurotoxin extensively investigated in neuroscience, neuromuscular physiology, and synaptic biology. Research focuses on its ability to temporarily inhibit neurotransmitter release, making it a valuable tool for studying nerve signaling and muscle activation.

Experimental models examine botulinum toxin in studies of peripheral nerves, synaptic transmission, motor neuron function, and neurophysiology.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Researchers investigate botulinum toxin for its ability to interfere with SNARE proteins involved in acetylcholine release at the neuromuscular junction, producing temporary interruption of cholinergic neurotransmission in laboratory models.
