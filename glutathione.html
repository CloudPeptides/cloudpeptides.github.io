Research Overview

Overview

Glutathione is an endogenous tripeptide widely studied for its central role in antioxidant defense and cellular homeostasis. Laboratory research investigates its involvement in oxidative stress regulation, detoxification, mitochondrial biology, and healthy aging.

Because glutathione is present throughout the body, it has become one of the most extensively researched molecules in cellular health and redox biology.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Researchers investigate glutathione for its ability to participate in redox reactions, neutralize reactive oxygen species, support detoxification pathways, and maintain intracellular antioxidant balance.
