Research Overview

Overview

IGF-1 LR3 is a modified analog of insulin-like growth factor-1 that has become an important research tool in studies involving muscle physiology, cellular growth, tissue repair, and endocrine biology.

Researchers investigate how prolonged IGF-1 receptor activation influences muscle adaptation, protein synthesis, body composition, and recovery.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

IGF-1 LR3 binds to the IGF-1 receptor, initiating signaling pathways involved in cellular growth, survival, and protein synthesis. Research continues to explore its role in skeletal muscle physiology and metabolic regulation.
