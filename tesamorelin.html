Research Overview

Overview

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). Researchers investigate how stimulation of the GHRH receptor influences endogenous growth hormone secretion and downstream physiological pathways.

Current scientific literature explores its role in body composition, visceral adipose tissue biology, endocrine signaling, and metabolic health.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Tesamorelin binds to GHRH receptors in the pituitary, stimulating the body's own release of growth hormone. Researchers continue to study the downstream effects on IGF-1 production, metabolism, and body composition.

Is Tesamorelin a growth hormone?

No. Tesamorelin is a GHRH analog that stimulates endogenous growth hormone release rather than being growth hormone itself.
