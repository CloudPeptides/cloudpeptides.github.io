Research Overview

Overview

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that has become one of the most extensively studied compounds in metabolic research. Scientists investigate its effects on appetite regulation, glucose metabolism, satiety signaling, and body composition.

By selectively activating the GLP-1 receptor, researchers explore how this pathway influences food intake, energy balance, and cardiometabolic physiology.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Semaglutide activates the GLP-1 receptor, a signaling pathway involved in appetite regulation and metabolic control. Research continues to investigate its role in energy balance and glucose homeostasis.
