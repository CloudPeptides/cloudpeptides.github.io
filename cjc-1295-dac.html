Research Overview

Overview

CJC-1295 DAC is a synthetic GHRH analog studied for its ability to stimulate growth hormone release through the GHRH receptor. The DAC component, or Drug Affinity Complex, is researched for its effect on pharmacokinetic duration.

Researchers investigate CJC-1295 DAC in relation to growth hormone physiology, IGF-1 signaling, endocrine regulation, body composition, recovery biology, and healthy aging.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

CJC-1295 DAC binds to GHRH receptors and is studied for stimulating growth hormone release. The DAC modification is researched for extending circulation time compared with non-DAC versions.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

What does DAC mean?

DAC stands for Drug Affinity Complex. It is researched for its effect on circulation time and pharmacokinetic duration.

How is CJC-1295 DAC different from CJC-1295 No DAC?

The DAC version is studied for longer duration, while the no-DAC version is researched separately for a different pharmacokinetic profile.
