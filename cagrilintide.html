Research Overview

Overview

Cagrilintide is a long-acting amylin analogue being investigated for its role in appetite regulation and metabolic physiology. Researchers study how amylin signaling contributes to satiety and energy balance.

Because it acts through a pathway different from GLP-1 receptor agonists, Cagrilintide is frequently investigated alongside other metabolic compounds in laboratory research.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Cagrilintide is designed to mimic the hormone amylin. Researchers investigate how amylin receptor activation influences appetite, food intake, gastric emptying, and metabolic regulation.
