Research Overview

Overview

Melanotan I, also known as Afamelanotide, is a synthetic analog of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH). Researchers investigate its interaction with melanocortin receptors involved in pigmentation biology and skin physiology.

Scientific literature explores its role in melanogenesis, UV-response pathways, and dermatologic research models.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Melanotan I is primarily studied for activation of the melanocortin-1 receptor (MC1R), a pathway involved in melanin production and pigmentation-related cellular signaling.
