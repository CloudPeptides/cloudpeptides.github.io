Research Overview

Overview

Melanotan II is a synthetic analog of α-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH) investigated for its interaction with melanocortin receptors involved in pigmentation biology and neuroendocrine signaling. It is widely used in laboratory research exploring melanocortin pathways.

Scientific literature investigates receptor activation, skin pigmentation biology, UV-response pathways, and broader melanocortin receptor function.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Researchers investigate Melanotan II for activation of melanocortin receptors, particularly MC1R and MC4R, which participate in pigmentation, neuroendocrine signaling, and related biological pathways.
