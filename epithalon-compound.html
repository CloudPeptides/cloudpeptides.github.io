Research Overview

Overview

Epithalon is a synthetic tetrapeptide commonly investigated in longevity and healthy aging research. Scientific interest has focused on its relationship to pineal gland biology, circadian rhythm, melatonin signaling, cellular aging, and telomere-related research models.

Researchers continue to study Epithalon in laboratory settings to better understand peptide signaling pathways associated with aging biology, endocrine rhythm, and cellular maintenance.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Epithalon is investigated for its potential influence on regulatory pathways involved in pineal function, circadian signaling, and cellular aging. Some research models also explore its association with telomerase activity and telomere maintenance.
