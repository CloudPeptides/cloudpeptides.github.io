Appetite & Energy Balance

Explore the biological pathways that regulate hunger, satiety, food intake, and whole-body energy balance. This chapter provides an educational overview of the primary hormones and brain regions involved in metabolic regulation.

Research Topic

Appetite Regulation

Appetite regulation is governed by coordinated communication between the brain, gastrointestinal tract, adipose tissue, pancreas, and endocrine system. Researchers investigate how these signals influence hunger, satiety, meal timing, food preference, and long-term energy balance.

GLP-1 Biology

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) is an incretin hormone released from the intestine after meals. Research focuses on its effects on appetite, gastric emptying, insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, and central nervous system signaling.

GIP Biology

Glucagon Biology

Glucagon is produced by pancreatic alpha cells and helps regulate blood glucose availability during fasting. Current research also examines its role in energy expenditure and multi-receptor agonist therapies.

Leptin

Leptin is secreted primarily by adipose tissue and communicates long-term energy stores to the hypothalamus. Research explores leptin resistance, appetite regulation, and body-weight homeostasis.

Ghrelin

Ghrelin is commonly referred to as the 'hunger hormone.' Produced mainly by the stomach, it rises before meals and stimulates appetite through hypothalamic signaling pathways.
