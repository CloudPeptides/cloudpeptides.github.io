Research Overview

Overview

NAD+ is a naturally occurring coenzyme found in every living cell and is essential for energy metabolism. It has become one of the most extensively studied molecules in aging and longevity research because of its role in mitochondrial function, cellular repair, and metabolic regulation.

Laboratory investigations explore NAD+ in models involving healthy aging, DNA repair, oxidative stress, neurobiology, and cellular resilience.

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Researchers investigate NAD+ as a critical cofactor in oxidation-reduction reactions that support ATP production, mitochondrial function, and enzymes involved in DNA repair and cellular maintenance, including sirtuins and PARPs.
